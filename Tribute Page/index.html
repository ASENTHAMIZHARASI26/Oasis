Biography

Kalpana Chawla (1962-2003) was an Indian-American
astronaut and the first woman of Indian origin to
go to space, serving as a mission specialist on Space
Shuttle Columbia for two missions (STS-87 in 1997 and
STS-107 in 2003). Born in Karnal, India, she earned
degrees in aerospace engineering from India and the U.S
later working at NASA before her selection as an astronaut
Tragically, she died along with six other crew members when
the Columbia disintegrated during reentry on February 1, 2003,
becoming a lasting inspiration for aspiring scientists and
engineers, notes a National Air and Space Museum article
